This page is the garden's inbox for ideas that are not ready to become full notes yet.

The rule is simple: if an idea has a clear claim, it can become a seed note. If it is still only a phrase, source, or question, it starts here.

## Active candidates

- [[garden/notes/academics-as-ai-managers|Academics as AI Managers]]: academics may be unusually well suited to AI work because project scoping and delegation are already central research-management skills.
- Researchers are not end-point verifiers: AI changes where judgment happens; researchers need to stay inside the reasoning loop.
- EM and variational methods: a short technical bridge between EM, lower bounds, latent variables, and variational approximations.
- Econ and math arguments about AI: collect citations and clarify what they miss about applied data science practice.
- Seminal statistics notes: work through Little, the Stanford journal club list, Hastie/Tibshirani interviews, and eventually a comparable ML ideas list.
- McCullagh through Cox: short foundational and applied stat notes on examples, protocol, population, sample, baseline, and applied judgment.

## Promotion rule

- `Note`: one clear claim or question.
- `Library`: source-guided reading or technical summary.
- `Pattern`: reusable workflow or teaching move.
- `Essay`: sustained argument that connects several notes.
