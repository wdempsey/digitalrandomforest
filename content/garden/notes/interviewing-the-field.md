At some point this project should include direct conversations with leading people in statistics, machine learning, and computer science about the state of the field.

That work should start early, even if the interviews happen later.

## Why do this

Interviews could help the garden do something writing alone cannot:

- capture oral history
- test emerging claims against senior perspectives
- compare how different communities define progress
- surface disagreements about rigor, openness, reproducibility, and education

## Candidate groups

- statisticians
- machine learning researchers
- computer scientists
- people who have led interdisciplinary data science efforts

## Themes to probe

- What has actually changed in the last ten years?
- What is being overhyped?
- Which statistical ideas remain central?
- What does AI genuinely change about training and practice?
- What should students learn now that they did not need ten years ago?

## Preparation work

- build a target list
- group people by lens: theory, methods, systems, education, management
- draft a core interview protocol
- identify which conversations should be public-facing versus background only

## Output options

- short garden notes after each conversation
- synthetic overview pieces
- one larger essay on the state of the field
