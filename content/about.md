## About

This site is a digital garden where I write about three things:

- how AI reshapes statistical reasoning and model design
- practical workflows for data scientists and teams
- the tools and systems I’m building to support that work

Much of the conversation about AI focuses on automation.

But in statistics and data science, the most interesting changes are happening somewhere else.

AI tools are becoming scaffolding for thinking.

They can help:

- generate alternative model specifications
- surface implicit assumptions
- explore unfamiliar methods
- automate routine coding and data tasks

But they still depend on human judgment about:

- causal assumptions
- identification strategies
- research design
- interpretation of results

In other words, AI doesn’t remove the need for statistical thinking - it makes good thinking more valuable.

This garden documents my attempts to understand that shift.

## CodexBatman

Many of the practical experiments behind these ideas live in a companion project: CodexBatman.

CodexBatman explores how AI tools can be integrated into structured, reproducible data science workflows.

Rather than treating AI as a chat interface, the project focuses on building systems that help data scientists:

- structure analytical projects
- manage prompts and reasoning steps
- maintain reproducibility
- integrate AI tools into research pipelines

The repository contains experiments, workflow templates, and tools that emerge from my own work using AI in research and teaching.

This site provides the conceptual layer behind those tools.

## Who this is for

This site is written primarily for:

- data scientists learning to work with AI tools
- statistics students exploring modern workflows
- researchers interested in reproducible analytical systems
- managers thinking about how AI changes data science teams

If you’re trying to figure out how AI actually fits into statistical practice - not just demos or hype - you’re the intended audience.

## Writing in public

This site follows the digital garden model rather than a traditional blog.

Posts appear in different stages of development:

- Seeds - early ideas
- Growing notes - partial explorations
- Evergreen essays - more developed arguments

Ideas evolve over time and posts are often revised as my thinking changes.

Writing this way has become one of the most effective tools I’ve found for improving my own work.
